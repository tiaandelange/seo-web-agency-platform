# Topical authority plan

Goal: make the site the obvious SA authority on **commercially-driven website development** — not on “marketing” broadly. Depth beats breadth; every cluster has one commercial hub page and supporting spokes that link up to it.

## Cluster 1 — Website cost & buying decisions (launch priority)
- Hub: `/pricing/` (+ `/website-packages/`)
- Spokes: cost guide article, choosing-a-developer article, custom-vs-template compare, maintenance-options compare; roadmap: ecommerce cost, redesign cost, hidden costs, hosting costs in SA.
- Why first: highest-intent cluster with proven rankability in this market (competitor cost guides rank and earn links).

## Cluster 2 — SEO-first web development (differentiator)
- Hub: `/services/seo-website-development/`
- Spokes: what-is-an-seo-first-website; roadmap: site not showing on Google, redesign without losing rankings, Core Web Vitals for business owners, schema explained for SMEs.
- Why: converts our method into visible expertise; supports every other service page.

## Cluster 3 — Lead generation for technical/service businesses
- Hub: `/services/lead-generation-websites/`
- Spokes: solutions pages (contractors, engineering, professional services); roadmap: what makes a contractor website generate calls, enquiry-form design, WhatsApp for B2B leads.

## Cluster 4 — Catalogue, RFQ and B2B commerce
- Hub: `/services/product-catalogue-websites/` with `/services/rfq-and-quotation-systems/`
- Spokes: manufacturers-and-suppliers solution; roadmap: catalogue vs ecommerce decision, RFQ workflow guide, pricing-visibility strategy for B2B.

## Cluster 5 — Business systems & automation (expansion layer)
- Hub: `/services/custom-web-applications/` (+ admin panels, portals)
- Spokes: website-vs-web-application compare; roadmap: spreadsheet-to-system signs, build-vs-buy for SME software, portal ROI; case studies are the primary authority assets here.

## Cluster 6 — Local (Gauteng)
- Hub: `/areas-we-serve/pretoria/` (primary), johannesburg (secondary)
- Spokes: local projects when permissioned, GBP posts; no new city pages without genuine evidence (rule 11).

## Operating rules

1. Publish order within a cluster: hub first, then spokes (spokes always have a hub to link to).
2. Every article names its supported commercial page in frontmatter (`supportsServiceSlugs`) — enforced by the SEO validator.
3. A cluster is “established” when: hub + ≥3 spokes live, all interlinked, hub receiving impressions in GSC. Only then open the next cluster.
4. Sequence: C1 → C2 → C3 → C4 → C5 (C6 runs continuously alongside).
5. Evidence-led content (case studies, screenshots, real numbers) outranks volume — prefer one proven piece over three generic ones (blueprint Phase 19).
