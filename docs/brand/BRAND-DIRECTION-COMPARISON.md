# Brand direction comparison

Prepared 2026-07-21. All three directions are complete, WCAG-prechecked, budget-compliant and **name-adaptable** (none depends on a final name). Details: `BRAND-DIRECTION-A.md`, `BRAND-DIRECTION-B.md`, `BRAND-DIRECTION-C.md`. Decision sheet: `FOUNDER-BRAND-DECISION.md`.

## At a glance

| Dimension | A — Technical Precision | B — Modern Systems Studio | C — Commercial Growth Partner |
|---|---|---|---|
| One-line feel | Engineering drawing discipline | Digital-product studio | Warm, conversion-first professional |
| Primary signal to | Contractors, engineers, manufacturers (segments 1–3) | Property/systems buyers, future SaaS clients (3–4) | Owner-operators, professional services, SMBs (1, 5, 6) |

| Ageing risk | Lowest | Highest (current-trend adjacency) | Middle |

## How each lands on key touchpoints

- **Homepage hero:** A — ruled headline block with a dimension-annotated diagram; B — dark product panel with interface montage; C — big warm type with orange "Request a quote" and a three-step outcome strip.
- **Service/package cards:** A — hairline-ruled cards, numbered; B — elevated component cards on grid; C — roomy cards with benefit-first headings.
- **Case studies (future):** A — drawing-style project sheets; B — screenshot-native system walkthroughs; C — before/after story format.
- **Proposal/quote PDF:** A translates best to documents (its aesthetic *is* documentation); B second; C weakest.

## Recommendation

**Direction A — Technical Precision**, with one deliberate borrow from C: adopt C's single-warm-CTA discipline (A's "survey amber" reserved exclusively for the primary conversion action) so precision doesn't cost conversion.

Reasoning against the alternatives:

- The launch battle is D2: winning technical/industrial website buyers from organic search. A mirrors that audience most strongly and is the hardest look for volume competitors to fake (their toolset produces gradients, not discipline).
- A carries the weakest photography dependency — decisive while zero permissioned client imagery exists (see `docs/content/EVIDENCE-REQUIREMENTS.md`).
- A has the most performance headroom and the lowest ageing risk; B is a strong second and remains the natural evolution if the systems business overtakes websites (a future re-skin B-ward is a token swap, exactly what the architecture was built for).
- C's warmth is better bought as copy tone + CTA discipline than as the whole identity; as the identity it undermines the niche signal.

All three remain fully specified for owner review; the recommendation binds nothing until `FOUNDER-BRAND-DECISION.md` is signed.
