## Tone-of-voice rules

1. Write like a competent engineer explaining to a business owner: plain, precise, unhurried.
2. Numbers and specifics over adjectives ("loads in under 2.5 seconds on mobile" beats "blazing fast").
3. Show the method, not the magic — explain *how*, never "trust us".
4. en-ZA spelling; ZAR with thousands separators; no exclamation marks in body copy.
5. Confident, never salesy: no urgency theatrics, no discount language, no "limited slots".
6. Respect the reader's expertise: technical buyers may know their domain better than we do — write to that.

## Wording that must be avoided

- "Innovative digital solutions", "cutting-edge experiences", "we bring ideas to life", "results-driven creativity", "passionate about pixels" — vague agency filler.
- Any ranking or traffic guarantee: "get to #1", "dominate Google", "guaranteed leads" (rule 10).
- Unsupported claims: "trusted by hundreds", "award-winning", "X% more leads" (until a verified case study exists).
- Cheapness signals: "affordable", "cheap", "from R999" (contradicts positioning).
- Startup-cute register: "supercharge", "unleash", "magic", emoji in copy.
- "World-class", "best-in-class", "leading" — unverifiable superlatives.
