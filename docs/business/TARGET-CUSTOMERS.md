# Target customers

## Primary segments (Direction 2)

### 1. Contractors and trade businesses
Civil, electrical, plumbing, roofing, waterproofing, dam lining, fencing, solar installers. Owner-operated, 1–50 staff, Gauteng-heavy. High job values; win work through referrals and Google. Buying trigger: “the phone must ring”. Budget R20k–R80k. Decision-maker: the owner. Objection: burnt before by a cheap site that ranked nowhere.

### 2. Engineering and industrial services
Consulting engineers, fabrication, machining, maintenance services, instrumentation. Credibility-driven buyers; need capability pages, project evidence, spec documents. Budget R30k–R120k. Decision-maker: director/BD lead. Objection: agencies don’t understand technical work.

### 3. Manufacturers, suppliers and distributors
Product ranges of 50–5,000 SKUs sold B2B via quotes, not carts. Need catalogue + RFQ, price-list control, dealer/stockist info. Budget R40k–R150k. Objection: forced into consumer-style ecommerce that doesn’t fit quoting.

### 4. Property businesses
Rental portfolio owners, letting agents, small developers. Need listing sites and management/tenant systems. Budget R30k–R200k (site → system). Objection: off-the-shelf tools don’t match SA processes.

### 5. Professional services
Attorneys, accountants, architects, medical/specialist practices. Local-intent searches, trust-heavy. Budget R25k–R60k. Objection: generic template sites look like everyone else.

### 6. Established small businesses (secondary)
Any SMB that has outgrown a DIY/template site and now wants leads. Entry point for the Starter/Professional packages; upgrade path to systems work.

## Explicitly not targeting

- R2k–R10k template shoppers (served by the volume market; unprofitable at quality).
- Enterprise tenders requiring large delivery teams.
- Pure marketing/social-media retainers without a build component.

## Shared characteristics used across content

South African; owner/director decides; value plain answers about cost and process; measure success in enquiries and quotes, not aesthetics; want one accountable technical partner; prefer WhatsApp/phone contact.
