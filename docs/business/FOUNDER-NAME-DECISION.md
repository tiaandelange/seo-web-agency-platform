# Founder name decision

Prepared 2026-07-21. Evidence: `FINAL-NAME-RESEARCH.md` (all checks dated, all provisional). Scores: `NAME-SCORING-MATRIX.csv`. **Nothing in the codebase changes until the owner decides and the official verifications below are completed.**

## Recommended name

**Datum Web Systems** — weighted score 4.35/5.

Why: engineering-native word that flatters the exact audience (a *datum* is the reference everything is measured from — literally the brand promise of measured, referenced work); "Web Systems" honestly spans websites *and* the admin/RFQ/portal expansion layer; trivially spelled and pronounced in en-ZA; **no company, agency or product conflict found anywhere in this round of checks** (2026-07-21); both `datumwebsystems.co.za` and `.com` showed no DNS. It is the only top-tier candidate with a clean conflict sheet in its own industry.

Risks to weigh: slightly sober/duller than "Modulus"; the DWS acronym belongs to a global asset manager (unrelated industry — just don't brand around the acronym); "datum" alone is descriptive, so trademark strength rests on the composite mark + logo.

## Strong alternatives

1. **Datumline Digital** (4.33/5) — same engineering root with a more coined, ownable feel and the strongest trademark posture of the set; `datumline.co.za` / `datumlinedigital.co.za` showed no DNS. Choose this if you prefer "Digital" (broader than "Web") and a more brandable word. Watch the "Dataline" mishearing on the phone.
2. **Modulus Digital** (4.20/5) — the most premium-sounding and most memorable, but carries the material conflicts: an active UK software company of the identical name (Modulus Digital Ltd, no. 14974459), `modulusdigital.com` already registered, and Modulus-branded companies (Media/Solar/Energy) already in SA in other industries. Choose only if you accept never owning the name internationally.

## Conservative alternative

**Highveld Web Systems** (3.68/5) — plainest and safest domestically: descriptive, no direct conflict found, effortless to say. The cost is a regional ceiling and weaker expansion signal. A defensible fallback if all engineering-word candidates fail official verification.

## Reject

- **Meridian Web Systems** — Meridian Dynamics (Cape Town, 2025) sells a near-identical "web systems & business automation" offer, plus Meridian Marketing Agency (CT) and Meridian Technologies (JHB). Keep only as the labelled placeholder; never launch under it.
- **Clearform Digital** — the word is saturated in software worldwide and `clearformdigital.com` is already a web agency.
- **Trueline Digital** — identical name, identical industry, UK (truelinedigital.co.uk). Usable in SA but never ownable; rejected in favour of cleaner options.
- **Bronberg Digital** — Bronberg Connect (Pretoria ISP) and Bronberg Dynamics (Pretoria engineering) create same-city adjacency confusion.
- Screened out: Vernier Digital, Gridline Digital, Setsquare Digital, Benchline Digital (conflicts documented in `FINAL-NAME-RESEARCH.md`).

## Identified risks (all candidates)

1. DNS absence ≠ availability: a parked registration without nameservers is invisible to these checks.
2. CIPC name conflicts are only discoverable through the official CIPC search — similar registered names (e.g. "Datum Projects", "Datum Consulting SA") may exist and were not visible in web results.
3. Trademark classes 35/42 must be searched on CIPC IP-online and TMView; common-law rights of unregistered SA traders are also possible.
4. Social handles unverified; LinkedIn company-name duplication is permitted by LinkedIn but hurts findability.

## Exact checks the owner must run before registration (in order)

1. **Registrar:** query `datumwebsystems.co.za` + `.com` (and the chosen backups) at a ZACR-accredited registrar; register on the spot if free (cost is trivial; do this first — domains move fastest).
2. **CIPC name search:** cipc.co.za e-services → name search for "Datum Web Systems" (and second choice); then name reservation (COR9.1).
3. **Trademark screening:** CIPC IP online search, classes 42 and 35, for "DATUM" composites; TMView (tmdn.org) for international conflicts; attorney opinion recommended before spending on identity.
4. **Google + LinkedIn:** search the exact name in quotes from a ZA connection; check LinkedIn company pages for confusable SA traders.
5. **Social handles:** LinkedIn company slug, X/Instagram (optional), and confirm a matching email domain plan.
6. **Phone test:** say the name to five strangers; have them write it down and search it.
7. Record every result with a date in this file, then instruct the rename (config swap procedure: `config/brand.ts` — one file).
